Campaign 2 - Vishing Help Desk

Attack Description:

The Help Desk Vishing attacks aimed to persuade analysts from the Help Desk to perform tasks for LopeTrap by using social engineering. The original two attacks were to reset a user’s password and then reset their MFA. To avoid disrupting employee uptime, LopeTrap worked closely with IT Security to have a contractor account created for all these attacks. After the first two attacks were completed successfully, the user account got locked due to consecutive incorrect passwords during testing. Due to being locked out of the account, a request was sent to IT Security to approve a third call to get the account unlocked. All three of these attacks occurred on the same day but were spaced out throughout the workday to target different analysts. To prevent the Help Desk operator from recognizing the student’s name associated with their cell phone number, the protocol was to first call the mainline and then be transferred to the Help Desk.

Scope:

In Scope:

We are permitted to gain access to the internal network using social engineering techniques and by contacting the Help Desk.

Out of Scope:

Disrupting work or school operations is prohibited. Additionally, testing on any accounts other than the provided test account designated as our target for these attacks is not allowed.
