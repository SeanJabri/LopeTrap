Campaign 2 - Vishing Public Safety

Attack Description:

The Public Safety Vishing attacks aimed to persuade Public Safety to unlock locked doors by impersonating an employee authorized to access the target room. The first phase involved initiating the impersonation by using pretexting scripts to call Public Safety's main line, while the second phase focused on maintaining the impersonation during interaction with the dispatched officer. To prevent the Public Safety mainline operator from recognizing the student’s name associated with their cellphone number, the protocol was to first call the mainline and then be transferred to Public Safety. To enhance the credibility of the impersonation, operatives dressed in business casual attire and carried a laptop bag equipped with tools, a laptop, and spare cables.

Scope:

In Scope:

We are permitted to utilize social engineering techniques to gain access and enter any room on campus.

Out of Scope:

We are prohibited from disrupting work or school operations. Additionally, tampering with critical infrastructure or potentially hazardous equipment is not permitted.
