Campaign 1 - USB Baiting

Attack Description:

We executed an on-campus USB engagement initiative targeting both students and faculty members. With a paramount focus on student welfare, we curated a PDF document and distributed it across 10 USB drives. Each drive housed a benign message, guiding the recipient to access a Google Form link embedded within the PDF. Upon plugging in the drive, users were prompted to participate in a brief survey via the provided form. Notably, Public Safety engaged in this process, contributing valuable insights as part of our comprehensive assessment endeavor.

Scope:

In Scope:

We were permitted to drop USB flash drives throughout Grand Canyon University's campus

Out of Scope:

Disrupting work or school operations is prohibited. Additionally, uploading any malicous file or content onto any device through the USB flash drive.

USB Baiting Attack:

Attack Preperation

Description:

In preparation for this campaign, we purchased 10 USB drives with a low storage capacity. This strategic choice was made to make them less desirable, increasing the chance of their return. Each of these drives was uniformly renamed 'CONGRATS!' and accompanied by a PDF file containing the message below. This measure was implemented to guarantee the absence of malicious content and to ensure strict adherence to our defined scope of operation.
